The Art of Handweaving

Our Aso-Oke is made on traditional looms using techniques passed down through generations, elevated with futuristic material engineering.

01

Raw Cotton & Silk Cultivation

Collecting premium organic cotton fibers and farming Anaphe wild silkworms to produce durable base threads.

02

Dyeing & Thread Spinning

Using natural dyes (indigo leaves, camwood, ginger) blended with modern high-lustre metallic polymers.

03

Setting the Loom (Oofi)

Aligning up to 10,000 individual warp threads on the traditional wooden loom to create intricate stripe grids.

04

Handweaving & Stitching

Weaving at a slow pace of 5-10 centimeters per hour, combining materials to build the iconic thick texture.
